Learn how to stay safe online with tips and guides on avoiding scams, protecting your privacy, and managing your security settings.

The internet is a valuable resource, but it's important to stay safe while navigating it. Here are some tips to help you protect yourself online:

  • Use Strong Passwords: Create complex passwords that include a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using easily guessable information like birthdays or common words.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Add an extra layer of security by enabling 2FA on your accounts.
  • Be Wary of Phishing Scams: Avoid clicking on suspicious links or providing personal information through email. Verify the sender before responding.
  • Update Your Software Regularly update your operating system, browser, and other software to ensure you have the latest security patches.
  • Secure Your Wi-Fi: Use a strong password for your home Wi-Fi network and consider using WPA3 encryption.

Scam Awareness: Protect Yourself from Common Online Scams

As more of our daily activities move online, it’s essential to stay vigilant against scams that target seniors. Here at StayConnect, we’ll keep you up to date with the most common online scams and provide practical tips to protect yourself and your loved ones.

Recognizing Phishing Scams

Phishing scams are designed to trick you into revealing personal information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or Social Security numbers. They often come in the form of emails, phone calls, or messages that appear to be from legitimate sources.

Tips to Protect Yourself:

  • Be cautious of unsolicited emails or messages asking for personal information. If the bank is calling you, they are not going to be in a rush to obtain any of your information. They have what they need and will be fine with you hanging up and calling them back.
  • Verify the email address and look for inconsistencies. If you don't know where the email is coming from, it's better not to open it at all. If you do open an email from an unknown sender, do not click on links or download attachments until you can verify the source.

Tech Support Scams

Another common scam is Tech Support Scams, which involve fraudsters posing as technical support representatives. They will often claim there is a problem with your computer that needs immediate attention.

Tips to Protect Yourself:

  • Remember, companies are not going to go far out of their way to offer support if you have not reached out to them first. You should hang up the call if you have not solicited it and remember, never allow a third party to take control of your computer unless you are certain they are legitimate.

Lottery and Sweepstakes Scams

Scammers may contact you claiming that you have won a lottery or sweepstakes that you did not enter, asking for personal information or payment to claim your prize.

Tips to Protect Yourself:

  • If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is. Do not offer personal information to unsolicited contacts. Legitimate lotteries and sweepstakes do not require payment to claim a prize. Remember, you have to play to win.

Identifying Online Shopping Scams

Fake online stores can trick you into paying for goods or services that do not exist.

Tips to Protect Yourself:

  • Research the seller and read reviews before making a purchase. A quick Google search can help you verify which websites are scams. Be wary of deals that seem too good to be true. You should not have to download a website's app just to shop there. And remember, legitimate websites offer legitimate payment method options such as major credit cards and PayPal. It's best to use a credit card when shopping online over a bank card. If the site you are on is asking for a wire transfer, that's a red flag that you should not purchase anything from that website.

Recognizing Bank Scams

One common bank scam involves receiving a phone call from someone pretending to be from your bank. They may ask you to verify your account by entering a temporary password they provide, which actually gives them access to your account.

Tips to Protect Yourself:

  • Banks will never ask for your password or PIN over the phone! If you receive this type of call, hang up and call your bank directly using the number on your bank statement or official website. Never give out personal information or verify details with unsolicited callers.

Protecting Your Privacy

Maintaining your privacy online is crucial. Here are some ways to protect your personal information:

  • Adjust Privacy Settings: Regularly review and update your privacy settings on social media and other online accounts.
  • Be Mindful of What You Share: Avoid posting sensitive personal information online, such as your address, phone number, or financial details.
  • Use a VPN: A Virtual Private Network (VPN) encrypts your internet connection, providing privacy and security, especially on public Wi-Fi networks.
  • Monitor Your Accounts: Regularly check your bank and credit card statements for any unauthorized transactions.

Managing Security Settings

Properly managing your security settings can help protect your online presence. Here are some steps to take:

  • Browser Security: Enable security features in your web browser, such as blocking pop-ups and preventing tracking.
  • Antivirus and Anti-Malware: Install reputable antivirus and anti-malware programs and keep them updated.
  • Regular Backups: Regularly back up important data to an external hard drive or cloud storage.
  • Secure Device Settings: Ensure your devices (phones, tablets, computers) are secured with passwords and, if available, biometric authentication like fingerprint or facial recognition.

Reporting Online Threats

If you encounter suspicious activity or believe you have been targeted by an online scam, take the following steps:

  • Report to Authorities: Report scams and fraudulent activity to local authorities and online platforms where the scam occurred.
  • Contact Your Bank: If you've shared financial information, contact your bank immediately to secure your accounts.
  • Update Your Security: Change your passwords and enable additional security measures on your accounts.
